Explanation: Grassborne talks about how simplicity can bring about a silent joy. Grassborne lies on the earth itself, happy in its bareness, while Snowchild is frozen, frail and longing in the midst of plenty. It depicts a person who has nothing but is never truly alone; a person who cultivates the same land that others ignore and refers to as paradise. Happiness already exists in the hands of those who live lightly, so it is not necessary to seek it out, as the poem reflects the wisdom of the lowly fisherman. For the persona, every dawn is sufficient, so they don't dream of grandeur. Because they have learned to dance in the flood, they laugh as storms pass rather than because they are spared. A reminder that happiness cannot be purchased, only remembered, Grassborne serves as a gentle protest against greed and dissatisfaction with its rustic imagery and carefree tone. It whispers about the beauty of remaining motionless in a world that is fixated on reaching farther.
